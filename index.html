Favorite Meals

Fried Turkey

Ingredients:
  • Turkey pieces (e.g., drumsticks, thighs, wings)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Garlic powder
  • Paprika
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Optional: Other spices like cayenne pepper for heat
Cooking Instructions:
  1. Wash and pat dry the turkey pieces thoroughly.
  2. In a shallow dish, combine the flour, salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika, and any other desired spices.
  3. Dredge each piece of turkey in the flour mixture, ensuring it is fully coated. Shake off any excess flour.
  4. Heat a generous amount of vegetable oil in a deep frying pan or pot over medium-high heat (approximately 325-350°F or 160-175°C). The oil should be deep enough to partially submerge the turkey.
  5. Carefully place the coated turkey pieces into the hot oil, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Fry in batches if necessary.
  6. Fry for about 6-8 minutes per side, or until the turkey is golden brown and cooked through (internal temperature of 165°F or 74°C).
  7. Remove the fried turkey pieces from the oil and place them on a wire rack or paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy!

White Rice Cooked with Coconut Milk

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup white rice (long grain or your preference)
  • 1 ½ cups coconut milk
  • ½ cup water (optional, adjust for desired consistency)
  • ½ teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • Optional: A small piece of ginger, a pandan leaf (knotted) for added flavor
Cooking Instructions:
  1. Rinse the white rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This helps remove excess starch.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed rice, coconut milk, water (if using), and salt. If using, add the piece of ginger and/or knotted pandan leaf.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat.
  4. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan tightly with a lid, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until all the liquid is absorbed and the rice is cooked through and fluffy. Avoid lifting the lid during this time.
  5. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it sit, covered, for another 5-10 minutes to allow the steam to finish cooking the rice.
  6. Fluff the rice with a fork before serving. Discard the ginger and pandan leaf if used.
  7. Serve hot as a side dish to your favorite meals.
